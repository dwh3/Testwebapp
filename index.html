Professional Experience

Lockheed Martin

The main project involved combining Business Intelligence tools, VBA, and batch scripting to generate reports by product area. These reports were automated to be sent to the inbox of area managers daily and displayed on screens throughout the facility. This allowed managers to spend less time generating reports while giving me visibility to manufacturers as to their performance.

The second project was a collaboration with the senior in-house Oracle developer. Part of the building process involved retrieving large amounts of small parts within a warehouse and putting them together in a kit. The application we developed allowed for reduced time in collecting parts while also allowing for the kit to be tracked throughout the process, including any parts not in stock but with which the process could be started without.

While I had a great experience at Lockheed Martin, I wanted to pursue a more generalizable toolkit. With this, my experience with Operations Research, and my newfound passion for computer science, I decided to pursue what I saw as the perfect blend of these: Economics.

Federal Reserve Bank of Boston

From the Spring of my first year of my Ph.D. to the Fall of my second year, I worked at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston in their Payments Strategies group. During this time, I worked on a variety of projects. The first was the 2019 Federal Reserve Mobile Financial Services Survey, which surveyed over 2,000 financial institutions on their mobile banking and payment offerings. The group had just finished the survey design when I was brought on. I was tasked with automating documenting the returned responses, cleaning the data, and analyzing it. Due to its sensitive nature, I used VBA for all of these.

The main project focused on payments fraud. I was tasked with developing a model of fraud types and mitigation strategies. I leveraged industry white papers on fraud types and mitigation strategies to do this.

American Antitrust Institute

From the Spring of my third year of my Ph.D. to the Spring of the fourth year, I worked as a Research Fellow at the American Antitrust Institute. This experience in policy advocacy was invaluable. I worked on a variety of projects, including:

  • A comment to the Attorney General on the proposed merger of Time Warner and Discovery, in conjunction with Public Knowledge, in which I rebutted claims of efficiency gains from past mergers and economic arguments made in prior filings.
  • A comment to the Secretary of Transportation, regarding the proposed code sharing arrangement between American Airlines and JetBlue. For this I reviewed the code-sharing literature and extended it to the case of these parties.
  • The main project was "Anticipating the Next Generation of Powerful Digital Players: Implications for Competition Policy," which aimed to identify the next generation of influential digital companies and the implications for competition policy. I was tasked with identifying these companies, using cluster analysis on product offerings to find counterfactual companies, and then evaluating the implications for competition policy.

Work in Progress

Research Assistant

If not working as a teaching assistant (and most semesters I am doing both), I am working as a research assistant. I have worked on a variety of projects, whick have allowed me to develop an eclectic skillset.

  • A current paper looks at the opening of Public Juvenile Mental Health facilities (CAPS) in Brazil on schooling outcomes. In doing this, our team is leveraging healthcare data from the Brazil Ministry of Health and schooling data from the Brazil Ministry of Education for over 3 million treated children, matching them with control units using exact matching techniques and an event study framework to evaluate the impact of these facilities. While the specific schooling and treatment data are sensitive, part of this project involved retrieving the geolocations of the facilities. A demonstration of how I did this using Google Maps API can be found here.

  • Another current paper, under review at an academic journal, evaluates the effect of the reformulation of OxyContin on Intimate Partner Violence (IPV) rates. To perform this analysis, my team needed records on reported incidents of IPV. To do this, I developed and implemented scripts to collect, clean, and merge controls into 30 years worth of FBI data, as the Uniform Crime Report reported.

Served as a liaison between professors and an undergraduate team of three in an inter-generational setting, effectively managing communication and expectations while promoting a collaborative and productive environment.

Publications

During my second summer of the Ph.D. program, I worked as a Research Assistant in a multidisciplinary team of political scientists and public health experts, studying the link between political polarization and health outcomes. While the group had already conducted a survey, I was tasked with conducting a literature review to justify a modeling framework to be used and implementing the model. The results of this work were published in two papers.The first was "The harmful effects of partisan polarization on Health," published in PNAS NEXUS. The second was "Bridging the Divide: Does Social Capital Moderate the Impact of Polarization on Health?" published in Political Research Quarterly.
